Tech Co-founder. Writer. Learner.

I am a developer who lives on the South Side of Chicago and project manager of mRelief, a website and text messaging based app that helps people determine their eligibility for public assistance and local resources. When I am not in a coding cave, I am in a coffee shop writing -- currently on Time Inc.'s payroll.

My professional qualifications include an internship at the White House and work experience at Google in marketing and accelerating campaigns of political influencers and nonprofits on Google+. I have a Master in Public Policy from University of Michigan and a BA in Political Science and English Writing Fiction from University of Pittsburgh. I am also an alum of Starter League's Web Development Course.

Outside of formal work, I am passionately involved in the work of BYP100 and I sit on the national and local Chicago board focusing on data and membership. Outside of social services and great prose, @mention me on posts about grants, Ghana, and awesome love stories.
